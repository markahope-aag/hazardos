# Floor Tile & Mastic Removal

**Proposal Title:** Asbestos Removal Proposal
**Hazard Category:** Asbestos
**Regulatory Fees:** Yes (DHS/DNR)
**Default Payment Terms:** 15 days

## Scope Statement

We hereby submit specifications and estimates for the removal of asbestos-containing materials.

## Work Practices

- Put up critical barriers (plastic) and signs to regulate the area
- Set up decontamination units, as necessary
- Seal all HVAC vents inside the regulated area (if any inside of it)
- Set up air scrubbers with HEPA filtration units to establish pressure differential (as applicable)
- Use of asbestos-labeled bags to dispose of the asbestos waste in an appropriate landfill by a third-party hauler
- Apply a lockdown encapsulant once the abatement process is done
- **If the floor tile adhesive to be removed is on concrete, we will use Sentinel 805 Envirowash to wash it.** Sentinel 805 Envirowash is a concentrated product specifically formulated for final preparation of subfloors following mastic removal on asbestos jobs and lead dust cleanup
- Workers wearing appropriate personal protection equipment

## Template-Specific Notes

- Baseboards/quarter rounds will be removed by others prior to our arrival if you are planning on reusing them. We cannot guarantee if we remove them, they will be able to be reused
- If there are toilets, refrigerators, or stoves present in the area where the flooring needs to be removed, please make sure to remove them before our arrival
- If the mastic removal occurs in the basement, sometimes the VOC fumes, which are less than 5%, may trigger the vapor sensor on your hot water heater (as sometimes happens with paint). Please note that {{company_name}} is not liable for any damage caused to blown sensors
