## Work Practices

- Confirm that adequate ventilation will be attained in the near future
- Before cleanup begins, the work area entrances should be isolated from the remainder of the residence and a plastic isolation chamber should be constructed to facilitate passage of workers from the outside into the work area without cross-contaminating the remainder of the residence
- The contained area should be set up with an appropriately sized depressurization unit (air scrubber in negative air pressure mode with exhaust piped to the outside)
- The vacuum(s) used to remove mold should be sealed true-HEPA unit(s). Lead or asbestos abatement vacuums are also suitable for this purpose
- Mold growth will be removed from affected surfaces using HEPA vacuuming or wire-scrubbing
- As a final step, apply a clear antimicrobial encapsulant to affected surfaces

### Encapsulant Disclaimer

Encapsulants are varnishes that seal any remaining spores that could not be removed. The benefit of encapsulating is that — should moisture return — any remaining mold roots or spores would be trapped under the encapsulant, reducing mold growth. However, if enough moisture or condensation is present, molds will make their way through the product and return. Therefore, while an encapsulant acts as a mold retarder, it does not completely prevent mold growth if humid conditions are present.
