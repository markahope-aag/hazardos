# Vermiculite Removal

**Proposal Title:** Vermiculite Removal Proposal
**Hazard Category:** Asbestos
**Regulatory Fees:** Yes (DHS/DNR)
**Default Payment Terms:** 15 days

## Scope Statement

We hereby submit specifications and estimates for the removal of vermiculite-containing materials.

## Work Practices

- Put up critical barriers (plastic) and signs to regulate the area
- Set up decontamination units, as necessary
- Seal all HVAC vents inside the regulated area (if any inside of it)
- Set up air scrubbers with HEPA filtration units to establish some pressure differential
- Dispose of the vermiculite in an appropriate landfill by a third-party hauler
- Apply a lockdown encapsulant once the cleanup process is done
- Workers wear appropriate Personal Protective Equipment

## Template-Specific Notes

- Over time and/or during the installation of the vermiculite insulation in the attic, some material may have migrated into other areas of the home, such as soffits, interior wall cavities, light fixtures, ceiling fans, and similar spaces. If {{company_name}} observes vermiculite in accessible areas during the course of work, we will remove and properly dispose of it. However, we are not responsible for vermiculite or related conditions in areas that are concealed, inaccessible, or not visible at the time of service
- **In summer:** Due to heat and/or humidity levels in the attic, we may have to work half a day or start earlier (7:00 AM). This may add an extra day to the removal. During a typical summer day, outside air temperature ranges between 95-97°F, but in the attic ranges between 120-160°F
- **In winter:** If we are to put a dumpster on the driveway, please have the driveway plowed. The dumpster company will not set the dumpster on the driveway for liability reasons
